"Mass-Produced" Yoyo

Designed a NASCAR-inspired yoyo with thermoformed and injection-molded parts, and manufactured 50 over the course of 48 hours. Design involved precise CNC machining for snap fits, accounting for tolerances and shrinkages involved in the various fabrication processes, and emphasized design for assembly.

CAD of the design. I was responsible for the production of 100 of the red hubcaps meeting a specified tolerance. I used 3-axis mill to produce the mold, performed thermal simulation of the injection molding process, and accounted for various design considerations such as part filling and ejection.

Results of a production run, after process optimization to minimize shrinkage and warping. Achieved a production cycle time of 46.67 minutes for 100 hubcaps.
